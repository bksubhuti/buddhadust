Aṅguttara Nikāya
Pañcaka Nipāta
VI: Nīvaraṇa Vagga

The Book of the Fives

VI: The Hindrances

Sutta 60

Dutiya Vuḍḍha-Pabbajita Suttaṃ

Hard to Find (b)

 

[78] [65]

Thus have I heard:

Once the Exalted One dwelt near Sāvatthī;
and there he addressed the monks, saying:

"Monks."

'Yes, lord,' they replied; and the Exalted One said:

"Monks, it is hard to find one,
gone forth when old,
endowed with five qualities.

What five?

Monks, it is hard to find one who speaks well,
it is hard to find one who grasps what is easily grasped,
it is hard to find one having the talent to grasp,1
it is hard to find one who is a preacher of Dhamma,
it is hard to find one who has the discipline by heart.

Monks, it is hard to find one,
gone forth when old,
endowed with these five qualities.'

 

1 Padakkhiṇaggāhī lit. grasping on the right. Comy. dinn'ovādaṃ padakkhiṇato gaṇhanto. The word recurs at A. v, 24, where the Comy. observes: yathā ekacco ovadiyamāno vāmato gaṅhāti paṭippharati vā asuṇanto vā gacchati evaṃ akatvā ovadatha bhante anusāsatha tumhesu anovadantesu ho añño ovadissati ti padakkhiṇaṃ ganhāti. See K.S. ii, 137; below V, § 156.
