Aṇguttara Nikāya
Sattaka Nipāta
V. Mahāyañña-Vagga

Sutta 43

Paṭhama Aggi Suttaṃ

Fire

 

[23]

[1][upal] THUS have I heard:

Once the Exalted One was dwelling near Sāvatthī,
at Jeta Grove, in Anāthapiṇḍika's Park;
and there he addressed the monks, saying:

"Monks."

'Yes, lord,' they replied;
and the Exalted One said:

"Monks, there are these seven fires.1

What seven?

The fires of passion,
hatred,
delusion,
the fires of the venerable,
the householder,
the gift-worthy,
the wood-fire.

Verily, monks, these are the seven fires.'

 

Childers. Aggi. Eleven (enumerated in Saṇkhyārtha Prakāça): rāgo — lust, doso — hate, moho — error, jāti — birth, jarā — decay, maraṇaṃ — death, soko — sorrow, paridevo — lamentation, dukkhaṃ — pain, domanassaṃ — grief, upāyāso — despair.

— p.p.
