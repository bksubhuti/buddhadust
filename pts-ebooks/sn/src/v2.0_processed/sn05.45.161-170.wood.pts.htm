7. On Longing

Suttas 161-170

6 Dukkhatā. Cf. S. iv, 259; K.S. iv, 175; Pts. of Contr., 316.

7 Khilā, fallow or unploughed land. At Vibh. 377 five ceto-khilā, viz.: Doubt and wavering in the Teacher, Norm, Order and training; peevishness, etc., to co-disciples. Not to be confused with khīlā (bar or post).

8 Nighā Comy. explains thus: 'A term for pains which destroy, in whomsoever they arise' (taṇ purisaṇ nihanti).

9 Vibhava (sometimes 'excessive wealth' = 'accentuated existence'). Cf. Dialog. iii, 208 and compare with § 1, where its parallel is brahmacariya. Thus it would here mean an annihilationist in view.

10 Tasinā (= taṇhā), cf. Dhp. 342. This is not in Sinh. MSS.
