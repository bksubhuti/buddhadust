# PTS Ebooks v2.0 Content Changes Summary

This document summarizes the high-level content-level refinements and translation updates between version 1.x and version 2.0 of the Buddhist Nikaya collections. These changes represent a significant editorial effort to improve the scholarly accuracy, terminological consistency, and overall readability of the texts.

## Key Content Improvements (Across all Nikayas)

- **Scholarly Transliteration Updates**: Systematic refinement of Pali terms to match modern scholarly standards (e.g., standardizing on `ṁ` and `ṅ`, and improving the rendering of technical terms like `Suttaṃ`).
- **Precision in Note Glosses**: Footnotes and editorial notes have been meticulously reviewed, with many Pali glosses updated for greater technical precision.
- **Enhanced Readability**: Strategic use of line breaks and paragraph spacing to better reflect the rhythmic and structural nature of the original Pāli, facilitating easier reading and chanting.
- **Vastly Improved Cross-Referencing**: Integration of a comprehensive row of links at the start of most suttas, providing immediate access to alternative translations (Bodhi, Thanissaro, Olds, etc.).

---

## Majjhima Nikaya (MN)
*v1.2 → v2.0*

- **Terminological Nuance**: Subtle refinements in English vocabulary to more accurately capture the depth of original technical terms.
- **Italicization Consistency**: Disciplined application of italics for Pāli terms like *Dhamma* and *devas*, ensuring a more professional and consistent scholarly presentation.
- **Structural Readability**: Improved line-break logic in long narrative sections to better assist the reader in following complex philosophical arguments.

## Anguttara Nikaya (AN)
*v1.1 → v2.0*

- **Technical Note Refinements**: Significant updates to technical terms within footnotes (e.g., refining `rāsiṅ` to `rāsiṇ` and `sankiṇṇaṅ` to `sankiṇṇaṇ`), reflecting current transliteration best practices.
- **Contextual Translation Tweaks**: Improved rendering of specific phrases to better reflect the nuanced context of the "Gradual Sayings."
- **Active Scholarly Links**: Footnotes that previously contained static text references now feature active hyperlinks to the referenced suttas, transforming the ebook into a more powerful research tool.

## Samyutta Nikaya (SN)
*v1.0 → v2.0*

- **Transliteration Standardization**: Systematic update of Pāli endings (e.g., `Suttaṁ` to `Suttaṃ`) and proper names (e.g., `Brahma` to `Brahmā`) throughout the text and headers.
- **Massive Expansion of Comparative Links**: Each sutta now serves as a gateway to multiple scholarly lineages through an expanded set of links to translations by Bodhi, Walsh, and others.
- **Note Integration**: Editorial notes (`edfn`) are more clearly distinguished and preserved, providing better insight into the translation process.

## Digha Nikaya (DN)
*v1.2 → v2.0*

- **Title & Header Refinement**: Subtle but important updates to sutta titles and introductory section headers (e.g., `Brahma-Gāla` to `Brahma-Gâla`).
- **Centralized Reference Row**: Introduction of a standardized row of links to alternative translations at the beginning of each sutta, significantly enhancing the scholarly utility of the collection.
- **Formatting for Flow**: Minor adjustments to paragraph structure to ensure a more natural and "book-like" reading experience.
